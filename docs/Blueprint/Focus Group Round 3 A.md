## Why Second-Layer Diagrams Are Critical

**Cognitive consistency**: If the Blueprint hooks people with visual discovery, breaking that pattern by jumping to text-heavy pages would jar them out of the exploration mindset.

**Scalable complexity**: A diagram can show relationships, flows, and hierarchies that would take paragraphs to explain. Users can grasp the mechanism at a glance, then read captions for details.

**Universal accessibility**: Your Gen-Z/Millennial audience and international users process visual information faster than dense text. Diagrams transcend language barriers.

## Suggested Approach for Node Diagrams

Each clickable balloon should open to:

1. **Primary Diagram**: Shows how this component works within the larger system
   
   + Flow arrows showing input/output relationships
   + Visual metaphors (gears, networks, feedback loops)
   + Key actors/roles represented as icons or figures

2. **Annotated Callouts**: Brief text labels directly on the diagram
   
   + 3-5 word phrases maximum
   + Problem → Solution → Outcome flow clearly marked

3. **Supporting Text**: Minimal, structured prose below the diagram
   
   + One paragraph explaining what you're looking at
   + One paragraph on why it matters
   + Clear "Next: Explore [related node]" navigation
