AreTéCracy flatly and firmly rejects simple majority as a basis for enacting law. Call it the **Five-One Fantasy**: the notion that 51% equals the will of the people. It almost never does, because it cannot. At 51–49, you are inside any reasonable margin of error — not just statistically, but morally. How does a rule that binds 100% of a populace become legitimate when nearly half explicitly reject it?

That is not democracy. It is what de Tocqueville called the *tyranny of the majority* — on a rotating schedule.

In a representative system, the uncertainty compounds at every layer. Your representative votes on your behalf, supposedly reflecting your will. But did they vote the way you wanted? Did you even vote for them? Did gerrymandering affect whether they represent your district fairly? Did a donor's call the night before the vote change their position? Did they trade a vote on this bill for a concession on another?

A Five-One result in a representative system does not tell you that 51% of the *people* wanted this outcome. It tells you that enough representatives voted one way that one count exceeded the other by at least one — each with reasons that are, at this point, literally unknowable. The probability that the result reflects political calculation more than the actual will of the people is too large to have a margin of error.

In AreTéCracy, a law that garners 51% of the vote — or anything close to it — is by definition a **bad law**. The goal of Ekklesia deliberation is **Homonoia**: genuine consensus; something as much of the population as possible can live with, even those who did not vote for it.

Think of AreTéCracy as an operating system for virtuous self-governance. The laws enacted by an Ekklesia are its software. Good software is well-architected, stable, and rarely needs emergency patches. Bad software — rushed, narrowly tested, passed on a 51% approval — requires constant maintenance and eventually crashes the system.

Homonoia is not naive idealism. Factions require division to survive. The system you have been living under was designed to prevent consensus from forming — because consensus removes the oxygen factions need to exist. The problem has never been that supermajorities are impossible. It is that you have been trained your entire life to believe they are, by a system that cannot afford to let them form.
