**Χρῶ τῷ Καιρῷ.** Use the kairos. Seize the moment.

The Attic Greeks distinguished between two kinds of time. *Chronos* is sequential time — the clock, the calendar, the accumulation of hours. *Kairos* is a different quality entirely: the right moment, the opportune instant, the brief opening in an opponent's armor that a prepared archer must take or lose. Homer used exactly that image. The gap opens; it will not stay open; you must be ready and you must act.

The democratic experiment Athens began was interrupted in 338 BCE at Chaeronea. Philip of Macedon's army won that day, and the experiment was suspended — not disproven, not discredited, simply stopped by force before it could finish becoming what it was trying to become.

Two thousand three hundred and fifty-nine years later, the conditions that made a resumed experiment impossible have changed.

The physical barriers of distance that once required a representative class have vanished. A citizen on the other side of a continent can participate in a live Assembly deliberation in real time. The engineering excuse for gatekept democracy — scale — is gone.

The rapid advance of AI has made one thing clear: whether AI will shape governance is no longer a choice. It will. It already is. The only question is *how*. One path leads toward a system that honors everyone equally — the foundational principle on which AreTéCracy is built. The other leads toward automated, high-surveillance, centralized control by an oligarchic few.

The MCR model will be replaced regardless. The question is what replaces it.

This is the kairos. The window is open. The arrow must fly now — not because we are impatient, but because windows close, and the people who benefit from the current system are not waiting to see what we decide.

**ΧΡΩΙ ΤΩΙ ΚΑΙΡΩΙ.**
*The moment has come.*
