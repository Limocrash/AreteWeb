Something most people never consider: in a representative government, you have no formal path to propose a law as a citizen. You can write to your representative, organize a petition, hire a lobbyist, or hope someone with access hears you. None of those are guaranteed.

In AreTéCracy, the path begins with you directly.

Any citizen who has an idea can submit a Hypothesis to the Office of the Ekklesia. There are no representatives or parties in an AreTéCracy, so you couldn't submit through one anyway. It could be anything: a problem to solve, a gap in current law, a better way to do something. Citizens, as Proponents of a Hypothesis, submit to coordinators for the Assembly itself, which by design serves everyone.

The submission process is designed to help you as a Proponent, not filter you or your voice in any way. IsoKratÓS — the Ekklesia's AI civic assistant — is available to help you develop your argument. It assists with structuring the case for the idea, identifying the strongest objections you'll need to address, finding comparable proposals already in the queue, and making sure the language is clear enough to be taken seriously. You don't have to use it, but it levels the field so you can have the same quality of preparation a professional advocate's team would (which is likely to be using AI for much of that task anyway, these days).

Before the Hypothesis moves forward, IsoKratÓS will cross-check with DikaiÓS to surface any obvious conflicts with existing canon or the AreTéCracy's Archegramma, or First Principles. This is precautionary rather than exclusionary. If there's a conflict, the citizen needs to know before spending weeks arguing for something the law won't permit.

One more thing that can happen at this stage is redirection. If a citizen presents a national-level hypothesis about something specific to their municipality, the Office may recommend it be directed to the appropriate geographic Ekklesia. Again, there is no intent to reject a Hypothesis. Instead it's being sent where it will have the most impact and the most legitimately interested audience.

Assuming the Hypothesis passes those safeguards, the next step is to schedule it for presentation before the Assembly. Coordinators for the Ekklesia take care of this for you.
