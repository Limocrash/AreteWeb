IsoKratÓS is a multi-layered name. The prefix *iso-* means "equal" and *kratos* means rule or power. The capitalized ÓS is the signature of all AreTéCracy AI models. And the name echoes Isocrates, the Athenian rhetorician who championed clear, honest argument over manipulative performance, and who believed the quality of public discourse determined the quality of public decisions. The name is a statement of purpose.

**For the Proponent**, IsoKratÓS is a civic preparation tool available from the moment a citizen begins drafting a Hypothesis. It can help structure the argument, research supporting resources, identify the strongest likely counterarguments and help the citizen prepare rebuttals, and even draft steelman versions of opposing positions so the proponent knows exactly what they'll be facing. It can help identify which Assembly has the appropriate geographic or domain scope for the Hypothesis — saving a citizen from weeks of deliberation in the wrong forum. The same preparation quality a professional advocate brings to a legislature is available here to every citizen, equally, by right.

**For the Ekklesia Coordinators**, IsoKratÓS is an administrative and triage tool. It helps the EC evaluate incoming Hypotheses for appropriateness, flag redundancies or potential mergers with existing proposals, assist with scheduling and session prioritization, and manage the day-to-day administrative work of a functioning Assembly. Coordinators running multiple Assemblies can use IsoKratÓS to sort and route submissions across them.

**During sessions**, IsoKratÓS becomes a vigilant listener. It monitors the floor in real time for logical fallacies and manipulative argument tactics: motte-and-bailey maneuvers, straw men, ad hominem attacks, circular reasoning, snuck premises, card stacking, and more than fifty other patterns it has been trained to recognize. It flags volatile or bad-faith language in steelmanning attempts; the rule requires good-faith restatement, and IsoKratÓS helps Coordinators confirm that standard is being met. It monitors live chat simultaneously, identifying decorum violations for chat monitors and elevating high-interest posts for potential floor attention. It performs real-time fact-checking, flagging weak or disputed citations to Coordinators for the Chairman's attention. And it tracks Axia-related activity throughout; civic conduct in the Assembly is part of the civic record.

What IsoKratÓS cannot do is equally important: it cannot mute anyone, ban anyone, rewrite proposals, or cast votes. Every consequential call belongs to the humans in the room.

It is a tool of the Assembly, never its master. That boundary is architectural, not aspirational.
