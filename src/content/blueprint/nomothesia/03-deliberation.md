Every session of the Ekklesia has Coordinators: trained facilitators whose job is to keep the Assembly running cleanly, fairly, and according to the rules the Ekklesia has set for itself.

Coordinators are civil servants. Their authority is procedural. Their job is to manage the session agenda in real time, working with IsoKratÓS to track where consensus is forming and where genuine disagreement still lies. When IsoKratÓS flags a fallacy or a weak citation, it is the Coordinators who bring it to the Chairman's attention, and the Chairman who decides how to respond. They monitor the live chat alongside the AI; elevating highly engaged ideas, identifying emerging objections, and making sure the floor and the room are working together rather than talking past each other.

The Steelmanning rule is their responsibility to enforce. Before any citizen may argue against a Hypothesis, they must first restate the proponent's argument to the proponent's satisfaction. Only then may they critique it. The Coordinators confirm this step has been completed before the counter-argument proceeds. It is a simple rule, and it eliminates an enormous amount of the bad faith that poisons modern political debate.

Coordinators are chosen through Dokimazo — evaluated for temperament, training, and track record before they are ever placed in a session role. The Ekklesia sets the terms of their mandate, and the Ekklesia can remove them. Like every Therapon in AreTéCracy, they serve the Assembly, not rule it.
