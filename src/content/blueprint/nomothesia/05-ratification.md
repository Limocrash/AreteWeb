*Thérmansis* means heating or tempering. The metaphor is deliberate and metallurgical. You cannot forge strong armor from cold bronze; you must heat the metal before you can put a hammer to it. This is the process by which a Hypothesis, once the Ekklesia has voted to advance it, is entrusted to the Aegis.

The Aegis is not anything like a parliament or congress and its members are not politicians. Rather, they are a team of experts, chosen through the Dokimazo process and evaluated for domain expertise, civic merit, and the capacity to serve the Assembly's intent rather than their own. The kinds of expertise various members, called Aegides, offer will depend on the domain of the Assembly they serve. An Assembly for health care issues will want to have physicians and public health specialists in its Aegis. An infrastructure Assembly brings engineers, environmental scientists, and municipal planners. Legal professionals are always included, since the end product must function as law. Their task is precise. They take what the Ekklesia approved in deliberation and translate it into language that is constitutionally sound, internally consistent, unambiguous, and as loophole-free as it can be made. They work with DikaiÓS — the legal AI — to check precedent, identify conflicts with existing canon, and surface language that might invite misinterpretation down the line.

The key constraint is that the resulting legal measure produced, for final vote, called a Psephisma, must be painstakingly loyal to the Ekklesia's original intent. The Aegis cannot change what the Ekklesia decided; they can only harden and refine the language that expresses it. If they encounter a genuine statutory conflict or a drafting ambiguity so deep that the Hypothesis cannot be armored in its current form, they send it back to the Ekklesia with specific questions. This can be iterative. The loop runs until the Psephisma is ready, not until a calendar deadline expires.

When the Aegis reaches Homonoic approval internally that the Psephisma has met that condition and is as well written legally as it can be, it is returned to the Ekklesia for final ratification. The citizens who proposed the idea, debated it, and voted it forward will recognize what comes back to them.
