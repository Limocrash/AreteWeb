*Dikaios* means just, or righteous. The capitalized ÓS is the signature of all AreTéCracy AI models. The name also echoes Díkē; daughter of Themis and goddess of divine justice without favor, depicted with blindfold and scales. The commitment it signals is simple: this AI exists to serve fairness in the process, not any particular outcome within it.

While IsoKratÓS works hand-in-hand with DikaiÓS through earlier steps in the pipeline by consulting it for case law, statutory precedent, and canon from the *pandektai*, the polis's corpus juris, its primary function is in Thérmansis: the Aegis stage.

**At submission**, when a citizen files a Hypothesis, IsoKratÓS prompts DikaiÓS for a preliminary legal review against existing canon and the Archegramma. This is not intended as a gatekeeping function but as providing information the citizen needs before investing weeks in preparation before deliberation. A conflict may be resolvable with a revision or might reveal that a prior law needs amending first. It may show the Hypothesis will have a better chance of advancement at a different level of Assembly. Better to know now.

**During Thérmansis**, DikaiÓS is the Aegis' primary research and legal intelligence tool. It assists with researching legal precedent, Archegramma compliance, and legal opinion across relevant jurisdictions. For domain-specific Hypotheses — healthcare, infrastructure, education, environmental — it helps collate and analyze the relevant domain research so the Aegides are working from a complete picture, not just what individual experts happen to know. It assists in evaluating draft language: detecting potential loopholes, identifying tangents that may require investigation or clarification, flagging ambiguities that could invite misinterpretation. When it identifies something the Aegis cannot resolve without more information from the Ekklesia, it helps make that case clearly. And when the Psephisma language is final, DikaiÓS proofs it — a last pass for consistency, precision, and statutory soundness before the document returns to the Assembly for ratification.

During sessions, when the Chair needs a quick legal read on something arising in debate — a conflict with existing canon, a question about whether a proposed amendment would require Archegramma review — the standard path is for the Chair to prompt IsoKratÓS, who then writes a context-sensitive prompt for DikaiÓS. IsoKratÓS already holds the full session context: the Hypothesis text, the arguments made, the specific point of contention. When it formulates the query to DikaiÓS, that context travels with it, so the response is targeted rather than generic. Direct access to DikaiÓS remains available for cases where the Chair specifically wants an unframed answer, but the orchestrated path is the default because it produces more useful output.

**Throughout the pipeline**, DikaiÓS provides database lookup functionality: Hypothesis records, Assembly schedules, Axia records, canon archive. It is always available as a reference tool for both the Coordinators and the Aegides.

What DikaiÓS does not do is make judgments. It does not determine whether a law is good or bad, wise or foolish, fair or unfair. Those are human questions that belong to the Ekklesia and the Aegis. DikaiÓS answers the technical questions beneath them: Is this consistent with the canon? Has this language created problems before? Is there a precedent that applies? What does the research actually say?

It is a tool for legal clarity in the service of democratic intent. The humans on both sides of the process decide what to do with what it finds.
