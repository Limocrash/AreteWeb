For standard administrative roles, the mandate takes effect immediately — a term defined, a scope established, an accountability structure in place from the first day of service.

For positions carrying greater authority, one additional stage precedes assumption of full responsibility. The selected candidate serves a complete apprenticeship term under supervision — operating within the full accountability structure but without independent decision-making power. At its conclusion, a focused evaluation answers a single question: ready, or not?

This is not distrust. It is the system acknowledging what the Dokimazo root already implies — that tempering happens in the forge, under real conditions, not in the abstract. A candidate who does not pass the apprenticeship evaluation does not necessarily leave the pipeline. The system has a long memory and uses what it learns.

What the Therapon holds at the end of this process is a mandate — defined, bounded, time-limited, and sealed with the authority of the Ekklesia. The pillar that holds it existed before they arrived. It will exist after they leave. They serve it. It does not serve them.
