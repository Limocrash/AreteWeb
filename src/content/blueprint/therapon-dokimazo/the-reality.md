The Dokimazo process has done everything it can do. Which is not the same as everything that needs doing.

Dokimazo selects for demonstrated virtue, confirmed qualification, and genuine civic fitness. What it cannot do is guarantee those qualities remain intact across years of authority, pressure, and the slow gravitational pull that power exerts on everyone who holds it. AreTéCracy's answer to that is not a better selection process. The selection process is already as good as we can make it. The answer is everything that happens after the vow.

No single mechanism carries the weight of keeping the gate closed. The system uses a stack, and each layer is independent:

**Mandates expire by design.** A Therapon's authority is bounded in time from the moment it begins. Power cannot accumulate the way it can when there is no expiration date.

**The Ekklesia veto never sleeps.** The citizen assembly retains the authority to recall a mandate at any time. This is a structural consideration the Therapon carries into every decision they make.

**GlaukÓS Mati** is reverse surveillance: publicly watching public servants, not privately spying on private citizens.

**IsoKratÓS** watches for what is right — citizenship, ingenuity, Aletheia, Philotimo. A Therapon who genuinely embodies the virtues AreTéCracy asks for will find that record growing richer with every term of service.

**Logodosia** has four layers. GlaukÓS Mati is the first. Sentilea — structured, scheduled reporting to the Ekklesia — is the second. Peira, a formal proceeding that convenes only when there is cause, is the third. Euthynia, the terminal evaluation at the close of every term, is the fourth and final.

---

AreTéCracy was built on the Virtues and for the Equalities, and those two things cannot be separated. When pleonexia finds its way in — and it is always looking — both erode together, not with a sudden collapse but with standards that slip one exception at a time.

This is where Aletheia becomes the constitutional mandate. A Therapon who never technically lies but manages carefully what the polis gets to see is failing Aletheia completely. The stack exists for exactly this reason.

And when the watching proves the trust was earned — when Euthynia closes the record and the verdict is *they served well* — the system that was rigorous enough to catch a Minotaur is equally rigorous in recognizing the Therapon who never became one.

The wax seal at the center of this map is not decoration. It is a reminder that stays current regardless of how well the system is working, because the moment it is taken as settled history rather than active warning is precisely when it stops doing its job.

The gate is closed. Keeping it closed is everyone's job, in every term, in every generation.
