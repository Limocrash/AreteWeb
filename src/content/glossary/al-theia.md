##### *Truth/Transparency*

*From Greek: a- (ἀ-, not) + lēthē (λήθη, concealment) = "unconcealed" or "not hidden"*

The fourth of the Four Aretai. Truth and transparency—not just factual accuracy but personal integrity, truthfulness free from pretense and self-delusion.

**Classical meaning:** In his famous "Allegory of the Cave," Plato described alētheia as what the prisoner discovered when he left the cave and saw, in bright sunlight, the actual objects that were merely shadows projected on a wall in his prior world. Pindar invoked it as the foundation of great virtue, guarding against falsehood.

**In AreTéCracy:**

- Lying to the polis is a high crime
- No functions of government shall be conducted "behind closed doors." Even high security activities must have reasonable oversight and transparency
- Law and policy must be based on verifiable reality, and with reliable data
- Commitment to truth is a key virtue at every level

**Implementation:**

- Logodosia (transparent accountability)
- IsoKratOS/DikaiOS verification and explainability requirements
- Open records and reverse surveillance
- Whistleblower protections
