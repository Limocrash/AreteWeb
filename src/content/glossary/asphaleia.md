##### *The Security Architecture of AreTéCracy*

*From Greek: asphaleia (ἀσφάλεια, safety / security / stability)*

The **security architecture** of AreTéCracy—protecting the system from capture, sabotage, and quiet decay without turning it into a police state.

Asphaleia covers multiple layers:

- **Physical security:** protection of critical infrastructure in the Survival Stack (power, water, data centers, comms, archives).
- **Cybersecurity:** hardened IsoKratOS / DikaiOS, audit logs, public verifiability of code and models where possible.
- **Institutional security:** rules against concentration of force, clear limits on emergency powers, layered oversight of enforcement agencies.
- **Information security:** defenses against mass manipulation, deepfake abuse, and algorithmic rage‑bait; transparency around recommendation systems.
- **AI alignment in context:** ensuring IsoKratOS, DikaiOS and related tools serve the Ekklesia’s intent, not vendor interests.

Asphaleia’s job is simple to say and hard to do: keep power **hard to steal, hard to hide, and hard to hoard**.

**See also:** Survival Stack, IsoKratOS, DikaiOS, Logodosia, Reverse Surveillance
