##### *Civic Merit*

*From Greek: axia (ἀξία, worth / value)*

A **dynamic civic merit score** reflecting how much trust a citizen has earned in specific domains of governance. Axia never replaces equal rights—it **weights expertise and integrity inside the field of equal standing**.

**Sources of positive Axia:**

- Demonstrated competence (tests, credentials, published analyses, successful projects)
- High‑quality contributions in the Pnyx (clear, evidence‑based, solution‑oriented Hypotheses, compromise proposals, honorable, Isotimía-centric arguments, respectful debate)
- Peer recognition and endorsements across factions
- Service in roles that passed Dokimazo + clean Euthynia / Sentilea reviews

**Sources of negative Axia:**

- Proven corruption, self‑dealing, or attempts to enclose resources or policies
- Systematic abuse of processes or misinformation
- Repeated decorum violations flagged by IsoKratOS

Axia can open doors to certain roles (e.g., Aegides, committee rapporteurs). It can also amplify the citizen's voice in relevant domains, giving him or her more weight in specialized deliberations

It should be noted that Axia: **Decays over time** if axiatic behavior ends or lapses, to prevent “frozen aristocracies” based on past good works and deeds.

**See also:** Dokimazo, Isopoliteia, Isegoria, Logodosia
