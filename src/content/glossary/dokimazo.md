##### *Vetting / Tempering*

*From Greek: dokimazein (δοκιμάζειν, to test/examine/approve)*

The vetting process for citizens entering specialized civic roles—magistrates, Aegides, key administrative posts. Combines integrity checks (past corruption, conflicts of interest, pattern of violating Isotimía), competence evaluation where relevant (domain knowledge assessed through testing and portfolios), and Axia review (existing civic merit in relevant domains).

The Ekklesia approves or vetoes candidate pools, then final selection happens via sortition (random lot) from the approved pool. This prevents both incompetence (anyone can enter) and capture (no one can buy their way in or use factional politics for the final selection).

For some roles, Dokimazo includes an apprenticeship period with Euthynia at the end of each phase—first to ensure the candidate is ready for full responsibility, then the normal end-of-term audit after their full office term is complete.

**See also:** Axia, Sortition, Isopoliteia, Euthynia
