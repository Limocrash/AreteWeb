##### *The Body of Citizens Making Up an Assembly of the Ekklesia*

*From Greek: ek (ἐκ, out) + kalein (καλεῖν, to call) = “the called‑out ones”*

The **body of citizens** who are called to exercise direct governance. In classical Athens, all citizens with civic standing could attend, speak, and vote directly on laws and policies. In AreTéCracy, the Ekklesia is the whole civic body; when it convenes, it does so in **Assemblies** that can be digital, physical, or hybrid.

**Modern implementation:**

- **Domain‑specific assemblies** (healthcare, defense, economy, education, etc.)
- **Geographic assemblies** (city, town, province, region)
- Coordinated, queued, and moderated through **IsoKratOS**

An **Assembly** is a particular sitting of the Ekklesia. The **Pnyx** is the space—digital or physical—where Assemblies meet.

**See also:** Pnyx, Isegoria, Isopoliteia, IsoKratOS
