##### *End-of-Term Audit*

*From Greek: euthynai (εὐθῦναι, straightening/setting right/auditing accounts)*

**Third layer of Logodosia accountability.**

End-of-term comprehensive audit of all magistrates and public officials.

In classical Athens, Euthynia was the mechanism that made officials MORE accountable than ordinary citizens. Every magistrate, upon leaving office, had to present a full accounting of their conduct, finances, and decisions to the Assembly. Citizens could bring charges—financial misconduct, abuse of power, or failure to fulfill duties. If convicted, penalties ranged from fines to loss of civic rights to prosecution. This created a culture where holding power meant enhanced scrutiny, not immunity.

**In AreTéCracy, Euthynia happens twice:** after the apprenticeship period (before a citizen begins their full term in a specialized role), and again after the full term is completed. This double-audit system ensures both that apprentices are ready for full responsibility and that those who complete terms are held fully accountable before returning to ordinary citizenship.

The process examines financial records, the quality of decisions, adherence to Ekklesia directives, and whether the official upheld the Four Aretai in their conduct. Evidence comes from Sentilea reports, Peira investigations (if any were triggered), Reverse Surveillance records, and citizen testimony.

**Outcomes of Euthynia:**

- **Clearance:** The official is judged to have served honorably and competently. Good civic standing is maintained or enhanced, potentially improving Axia and future Dokimazo eligibility.
- **Sanctions:** Penalties for misconduct that doesn't rise to criminal level—fines, Axia reductions, public censure, negative impact on future Dokimazo vetting.
- **Disqualification:** The official is barred from future civic roles for serious failures of judgment or ethics. Significant Axia penalties and Dokimazo disqualification in relevant domains for a period determined by the Ekklesia—typically aligned with the Ostracism time limit (default: 10 years). This gives citizens a chance to return to civic life after demonstrating reform.
- **Prosecution:** Criminal referral if evidence shows lawbreaking—at which point regular Isonomia processes take over.

Euthynia is not revenge or punishment theater. It's the systematic enforcement of the principle that power comes with enhanced responsibility. If you want the honor of serving the polis, you accept the scrutiny that comes with it—not just during your term, but after, when you can no longer hide behind your office.
