##### *AreTéCracy Reverse-Surveillance Architecture (“Democracy’s Watchful Eye”)*

*Etymology.* From Greek *glaukós* (γλαυκός), “gleaming, bright‑eyed, bluish‑green,” associated with Athena’s epithet *Glaukopis* (“bright‑eyed”), and modern Greek *máti* (μάτι), “eye,” also the term for the protective *mati* talisman (evil‑eye amulet).

*Definition.* Glaukós Mati denotes AreTéCracy’s reverse-surveillance system, designed to monitor holders of public power rather than ordinary citizens. Operationally, it comprises a network of recording and analytic mechanisms that generate cryptographically tamper‑evident logs of official actions, decisions, and communications, forming the first tier of the platform’s Logodosia (account‑giving) framework.

*Symbolism.* The system is personified by Nyctimene, an AI‑augmented rendering of the *Glaux* (Little Owl of Athens), the classical emblem of Athenian wisdom, prudence, and civic guardianship. By fusing the owl’s watchful gaze with the cultural resonance of the *mati* talisman, Glaukós Mati reframes surveillance as a democratically constrained safeguard against pleonexia, corruption, and emerging forms of tyranny, rather than as an instrument of social control.

*Cross‑references.* See: Logodosia; Reverse Surveillance; Alētheia; Pleonexia.
