##### *Indictment for Unconstitutional Proposal*

*From Greek: graphē (γραφή, indictment) + para (παρά, against) + nomos (νόμος, law)*

Ancient Athenian legal procedure where any citizen could challenge a proposed or passed law as violating fundamental principles. If the Ekklesia agreed, the law was struck down and the proposer could face penalties.

**In AreTéCracy:** The legal embodiment of Isegoria—every citizen can challenge any law, policy, or even Archegramma amendments as violating Isotimía or the Four Isótēs. This right exists during Nomothetēsis (before passage), after ratification (within review period), and against magistrate actions.

The Ekklesia hears arguments and decides via Stereohomonoia (solid consensus, 90%+ at minimum). If the challenge succeeds, the law is nullified. Egregious violations may result in sanctions against the proposer.

**Modern parallel:** US citizens can challenge laws as unconstitutional in court. AreTéCracy makes this more direct—the people themselves decide via Ekklesia, not unelected judges.

Without graphē paranomon, the Archegramma is just words. This gives citizens teeth to defend their own constitutional principles.
