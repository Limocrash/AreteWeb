##### *Distributed Productive Capacity*

*Named for Hephaistos (Ἥφαιστος), god of craft, technology, and the forge*

Economic foundation pillar ensuring survival stack essentials—energy, communications, food, transportation, payments—cannot be monopolized by pleonektai. Restores distributed productive capacity through the **Workshop Economy**: many small enterprises, worker cooperatives, and local production replacing corporate feudalism.

**The Workshop Economy framework:**

- **Worker ownership models** - Cooperatives, employee-owned businesses, low CEO/worker pay ratios (20:1, not 300:1)
- **Small enterprise support** - Functional SBA-style loans, public capital for tools/training, maker spaces and tool libraries
- **Anti-monopoly enforcement** - Breaking up oligopolies through liturgies tax incentives (lower rates for those who break up and reinvest in public benefit), actual antitrust action
- **Local production** - Manufacturing, repair, food systems owned by communities, not distant corporations
- **Public procurement reform** - Favoring distributed small businesses over vendor capture
- **Infrastructure neutrality** - Essential pipes (cloud, payments, logistics) can't be weaponized

**Why this matters:** When three companies control a sector, it's not a market—it's feudalism. Citizens become rent-payers hoping their lords stay benevolent. Workshop Economy breaks pleonektai stranglehold by building UP distributed capacity across every sector, especially survival stack.

**Current trajectory vs. Workshop Economy:**

- **Current:** Five megacorps own everything, citizens are economic serfs
- **Workshop:** Thousands of small enterprises, citizens are economic participants

Adam Smith's Invisible Hand worked because power was distributed across many butchers, bakers, and brewers. When power concentrates, markets become extraction mechanisms. Workshop Economy restores the conditions making markets serve people.

**Future-proofing (ATD):** As AI/automation advance, Workshop Economy ensures tools remain distributed, preventing oligarchic ownership of productive capacity. See ATD for long-term automation response.
