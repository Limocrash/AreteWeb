##### *Equal Right to Speak*

*From Greek: isos (ἴσος, equal) + agoreuein (ἀγορεύειν, to speak in the assembly)*

One of the Four Isótēs (pedestals of equal standing). Every citizen has the same formal right to address the Assembly—no matter their wealth, fame, or faction.

In practice, truly equal voice needs **structure**, or the loudest and richest (pleonektai) take over.

**Implementation in AreTéCracy:**

- **IsoKratOS** manages queues, timeboxing, and topic focus.
- Session agendas are built from submitted Hypotheses and citizen priorities, there are no parties or party whips.
- **Open‑floor time** is queued transparently (first‑come‑first‑served by default, with optional randomization to prevent queue‑gaming).
- **Axia** can *amplify* time for demonstrably informed contributors in a domain—but cannot silence others.
- **Misthos** (civic pay) and **digital access** guarantees ensure that poor or time‑constrained citizens can still show up and be heard.
