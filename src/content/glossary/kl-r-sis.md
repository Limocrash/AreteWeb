##### *Sortition / Random Lots*

*From Greek: klērōsis (κλήρωσις, allotment by lot)*

Random selection from a qualified pool. Used in Athens to prevent political bias or permanent political classes. In AreTéCracy, klērōsis remains a **core anti‑pleonexia tool**, but always **combined** with vetting.

**Modern use:**

- Always downstream of **Dokimazo + Axia and Ekklesia approval**:

  1. Dokimazo + Axia - First, build a vetted pool based on competence and reputation of integrity.

  2. Ekklesia Approval - The Assembly approves the pool.

  3. Klērōsis - Sortition chooses who actually serves.

**Applications:**

- Citizen juries, review boards, oversight panels.
- Magistrate-level administrative roles (with term limits and Euthynia).
- Judicial panels and Justices:
  - Candidate lists initially proposed by an expert panel (judges + attorneys),
  - Ratified by the Ekklesia,
  - Final composition by sortition from that ratified list.

This keeps roles **accessible** to ordinary citizens while preventing both hereditary elites and pure randomness from wrecking the system.

**See also:** Dokimazo, Axia, Isopoliteia, Euthynia
