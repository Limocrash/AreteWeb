##### *Ratified Canonic Law*

*From Greek Nomos (Νόμος) - “Law” in the formal, canonical sense.*

 In AreTéCracy, a Nomos is a Psephisma (bill) that has:

1. Been certified by the Aegides as constitutional, enforceable, and faithful to the Ekklesia’s declared intent, and
2. Passed by the Ekklesia in a Homonoic (or designated supermajority) vote.

Only at that point does a proposal leave the Hypothesis/Psephisma cycle and enter the body of binding law. All Nomos should be intended for permancence over time, but are always refactorable.
