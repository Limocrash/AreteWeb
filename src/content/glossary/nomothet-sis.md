##### *Process of Creating Legal Policies*

*From Greek: nomos (νόμος, law) + thesis (θέσις, placing/setting)*

The lawmaking pathway from citizen idea to enacted law.

**Quick outline of process:**

1. **Hypothesis** (Ὑπόθεσις) - Citizen proposes idea
2. **Thérmansis** (Θέρμανσις) - Vetting and tempering of the proposal
3. **Deliberation** - Ekklesia debate via IsoKratOS
4. **Aegides drafting** - Legal professionals craft precise language via DikaiOS to create a Psephisma
5. **Ratification** - Ekklesia votes (Homonoia threshold if major)
   ---***OR*** ---
6. **Iteration** -  If the vote fails to reach Homonoia, the Ekklesia works to negotiate compromise, changes to address concerns of minorities, etc., or proposes software development-style methods for discovery, such as A/B testing or limited pilots in small areas, then returns Hypothesis to Aegides.
7. **Psephisma becomes Nomos (canon)** - Enacted law with rationale documented

**See also:** Hypothesis, Thérmansis, Aegides, Psephisma, Homonoia
