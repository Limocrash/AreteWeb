##### *Assembly Space (Digital/Physical Hybrid)*

*From Greek: Pnyx (Πνύξ) - the hill in Athens where the Ekklesia met*

**Definition:** The Pnyx is the space—digital, physical, or hybrid—where Assemblies (Ekklesia sessions) convene for deliberation and decision-making. In ancient Athens, the Pnyx was the hillside gathering place for citizen assemblies. In AreTécracy, the Pnyx is the structured decision-room that enables Isegoria (equal voice) at scale.

**Modern Implementation:** The AreTécracy Pnyx operates as a hybrid system:

- **Digital participation** (default): Remote/asynchronous access via IsoKratOS platform
- **Physical assemblies** (optional): In-person sessions with simultaneous remote participation
- **Unified queue**: No second-class citizens—remote and physical participants have equal standing

**The Hybrid Pnyx Principle:** If physical assemblies exist, they must be hybrid by design. Physical room + screens + unified speaker queue + remote pathway = equal standing regardless of participation mode. This is "civic work-from-home."

**Contrast with Agora:** Earlier drafts called this the "Digital Agora," but Pnyx is more precise. The Agora was the marketplace/public square (general gathering). The Pnyx was specifically the decision-making space for assemblies.

**The Phoenix Protocol:** The strategy to establish new Pnyx systems for local government units (LGUs) or nations, piloting them in parallel to existing structures so organized self-governance can be ready when current systems fail.

**See also:** Ekklesia, Isegoria, IsoKratOS, Assembly, Phoenix Protocol
