##### *Finalized Bill Ready for Referendum*

From psēphos (ψῆφος), “pebble” or “to vote on.” A *Psephisma* is the **final bill text that is formally brought up for vote** to become law. It is the shaped, legally structured proposal that emerges from the dialogue between the Ekklesia and the Aegides.

In AreTéCracy, the pipeline looks like this:

- **Hypothesis** — A refined proposal from the Ekklesia, defining their intent in human terms.

- **Psephisma** — The Aegides translate that intent into full legal architecture: clauses, definitions, constraints, enforcement mechanisms, compliance with the Archegramma and precedent, etc. This text is then returned to the Ekklesia **for a vote.**

- **Nomos** — Once a Psephisma passes Homonoically **at both levels**—

  - the Aegides certify that it is constitutional, enforceable, and faithful to stated intent, and
  - the Ekklesia gives final homonoic approval—

  it becomes **Nomos**, canonical law.

The process is deliberately **iterative**. If a Psephisma fails either test—the Aegides cannot certify it, or the Ekklesia is not prepared to grant Homonoia—then it returns to an **Agile-style loop**:

* revise the Hypothesis,

* adjust the Psephisma,

* re-clarify intent, and try again.

Be creative. Try innovative changes, perform A/B tests, try small pilots, hold focus groups. Laws are treated less like stone tablets dropped from the sky and more like carefully iterated releases: small enough to understand, testable in the real world, and always open to refactor through future Hypotheses and Psephismata.

**See also:** Hypothesis, Nomos, Aegides, Archegramma, Homonoia, DikaiOS, Asphaleia.
