The set of systems a modern human needs to stay alive **and** function in society. If any major layer of the Survival Stack fails or is captured, ordinary citizens are no longer meaningfully free.

In AreTéCracy, this is **not just “the economy”**—it’s the baseline infrastructure that must never be turned into a private choke point.

**Core layers typically include:**

- **Physical essentials**
  - Food production and distribution
  - Clean water and sanitation
  - Energy (electricity, heating / cooling fuels)
  - Basic housing and transport
- **Social essentials**
  - Healthcare and medicine
  - Public safety and emergency response
  - Basic education
- **Digital & institutional essentials**
  - Communications networks (internet, mobile)
  - Core financial rails and payments
  - Identity and civic‑access systems
  - Foundation AI models and core compute once they become necessary to function in society

**Its importance in AreTéCracy:**

- Whoever **owns or controls** any substantial portion of the Survival Stack effectively owns everyone who depends on it.
- IsoEudaimonia **fails in practice** if access to these systems is priced as a luxury, gated against free competition by private monopolies, or turned into a political tool.
- Therefore, Isotímia and democracy fail as well.

AreTéCracy does **not** recommend or require state ownership of the Survival Stack, that is the choice of an Ekklesia; but if privately held it does require:

- Public options and hard anti‑monopoly constraints,
- Transparent licensing and oversight (especially for publicly funded R&D),
- Constitutional limits preventing pleonektai from enclosing it.
