##### *Public Servant / Administrator*

*From Greek: therapōn (θεράπων, attendant/servant/caretaker)*

A public servant who performs administrative or specialized work for the polis. AreTéCracy distinguishes between routine administrative functions and positions wielding significant authority:

**Standard Therapon:** Performs routine administrative tasks—clerks, technicians, specialists who keep government functioning. Each Ekklesia determines hiring approach (permanent employees, rotating contractors, term-limited civil servants, etc.), Dokimazo requirements, and accountability level appropriate to their context. These roles execute policy rather than make significant decisions.

**Magistrate (Therapōn Archōn):** Holds significant administrative authority requiring throrough Dokimazo vetting. Selected via sortition from pre-vetted professional pools. Subject to full Logodosia accountability (all four layers). Includes Aegides (legislative drafters), department heads, major program managers, enforcement officials.

**Common patterns (each Ekklesia adapts):**

- Magistrates typically serve time-limited stints (6 months to 3 years depending on complexity)
- May return for non-consecutive terms (recommended lifetime cap: 9-12 years total)
- Dokimazo Corps maintains qualified pools and rotates to prevent capture
- Standard Therapons often have term limits or rotation requirements to reduce entrenchment risk, but structure varies by local needs and trust levels

The core principle: power demands accountability proportional to authority. How each community implements that—permanent civil service, rotating contracts, hybrid models—depends on their context, culture, and comfort with institutional stability vs. anti-capture measures.
