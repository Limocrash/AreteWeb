##### *Distributed Small Enterprise Model*

Economic framework where productive capacity is distributed across many small enterprises—workshops, cooperatives, family businesses, modest-sized manufacturing plants, guilds—rather than consolidated in corporate oligopolies.

**The actual workshops:** Local manufacturing, repair shops, maker spaces, artisan production, service businesses, food cooperatives, small-scale factories. Think many butchers, bakers, and brewers (Adam Smith's original vision, not five megacorps controlling entire sectors).

**Support mechanisms:**

- **Financing:** Functional small business loans (pre-capture SBA model), public capital grants, revenue-shared advances (pay back only if successful)
- **Worker cooperatives:** Employee ownership, profit-sharing, democratic workplace governance
- **Guilds and apprenticeships:** Skill transmission, quality standards, mutual support networks
- **Maker spaces and tool libraries:** Shared expensive equipment, training, community workshops
- **Public procurement preferences:** Government contracts favor distributed small businesses over consolidated vendors

**Athens precedent:** Used lottery system (kleroterion) for some funding decisions—random selection among qualified applicants prevented "who you know" favoritism and ensured weird ideas got funded alongside obvious ones.

**Goal:** Break pleonektai stranglehold by building UP distributed capacity. Many small fires beat one glass tower. The Invisible Hand only works when the market has many hands.
