In the current system, money buys access, and access buys the political system. We end this with *Isotímia* (Equal Honor). We don't disparage wealth, but we recognize that the *Pleonektai* (those driven only to seek more for themselves) often use it to extract opportunity from the community.

Here, lobbying is impossible because there are no politicians to lobby. Campaign donations are impossible because there are no campaigns. Your voice is weighted by your citizenship, not your net worth. The only question before the lawmaking body — which is you — is what laws are good for everyone, or as close to that goal as can possibly be achieved.

Think about what it would actually cost to “buy” a law in the AreTéCracy model. In an MCR (mixed constitution republic or so-called “democratic republic”) all a corporation or billionaire needs to do is buy enough lawmakers’ votes to get to 51%. It can sometimes be done for just hundreds of thousands of dollars, but it might take millions or even tens of millions. These wealthy people can afford that easily for a law that helps them make more money. But in AreTéCracy the lawmakers *are the people*. AreTéCracy pays its citizens misthos — civic compensation — to participate fully in self-governance. That means the entire eligible electorate shows up and votes, not just the party faithful, and they must reach consensus, or Homonoia, for a proposal to pass. To pass even one unpopular law, you wouldn't be bribing a faction. You'd need to buy enough actual voters, over their own consciences, the value of their misthos and citizenship rights if they get caught, and the fact that they and everyone they know would have to live under a law they actually didn’t like, to achieve what every citizen knows is a “false consensus.” 

Let’s calculate the cost for a country the size of, say, the USA, that had a VEP (Voting-Eligible Population) of around 240 to 245 million as of the 2024 election. To buy 70% of all eligible voters in the polis, you’d end up paying off 170 million people or more. That’s almost the entire number of people that actually voted in 2024\! At even a modest price per vote for a law people genuinely don't want, you could be looking at a trillion dollars or even more. No billionaire on earth could afford that. Scale it to any country you like; the basic math doesn't change. No billionaire is rich enough to buy even one law, and no law could provide enough in stolen profit or tax cuts for the rich to ever make back the “investment cost” of the bribe.

*But think about what would happen if they actually did: Talk about redistribution of wealth\!*
