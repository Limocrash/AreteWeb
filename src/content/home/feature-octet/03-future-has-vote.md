We are the first system to give those too young to vote and yet to be born generations after us a seat at the table.

When any generation gives in to the temptation to live beyond its means and leave the bill to people who can't vote yet, it is a heinous form of *taxation without representation* against our own descendants

Every proposed law is stress-tested for its long-term impact by multiple failsafes. If a policy risks creating short-term gain at a future cost, the **Generational Fairness Doctrine** intervenes. This doctrine requires the Return on Investment (ROI) of any new policy to **exceed its tax burden** before the next generation takes the reins, ensuring the economic benefit pays for itself and leaves every successor generation better off than the last.

This principle of generational responsibility is captured by AreTéCracy's hero of choice: \
*"Our path from here is beyond the stars. Let us always pass the flame with honor, dignity, and respect, leaving behind every blessing we can; that they may remember us for the good we have done and not for harm."*

*— Theseus*
