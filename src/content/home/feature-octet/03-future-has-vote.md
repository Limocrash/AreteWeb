We are the first system to give those too young to vote and yet to be born generations after us a seat at the table.

When any generation gives in to the temptation to live beyond its means and leave the bill to people who can't vote yet, it is a heinous form of *taxation without representation* against our own descendants.

Every proposed law in AreTéCracy is stress-tested for its long-term impact. There are multiple failsafes to protect our future generations from unnecessary burden and tragic loss. One is that, if a policy creates short-term gain at a future cost, the Generational Fairness Doctrine intervenes. The doctrine requires that the ROI of any new policy must exceed its tax burden before the next generation takes the reins. This way the economic benefit of every policy pays for itself, leaving every successive generation better off than the last.

To quote our co-founder:

*"Our path from here is beyond the stars. Let us always pass the flame with honor, dignity, and respect, leaving behind every blessing we can; that they may remember us for the good we have done and not for harm."*
