AreTéCracy proposes that there should be a new guaranteed right for every citizen: That the right to "flourish as a human," as Aristotle put it, or the "pursuit of happiness," as Locke and Jefferson put it, must be preserved in both spirit and law. To be clear, it doesn't stop anyone from achieving what they can on their own merits; it simply assures that what we call the Survival Stack is equally accessible to all.

We all already know what needs to end. There can never again be "too big to fail." One centi-billionaire can't control the lion's share of online commerce. Who can use one third of the satellites in low-earth orbit can't be up to the whims of another. And technologies that will drive the advancement of the human experience to a true post-scarcity world will only do so if they aren't in the hands of a handful of self-interested oligarchs.

Furthermore, financial institutions need to be held responsible for their actions rather than play roulette with our deposits and depend on bailouts when the ball drops on zeros. Minimum standards of good healthcare, childcare, housing, food and education need to be established and the means found to ensure them to every citizen. Infrastructure must be well-designed, well-built, well-financed, and well-purposed. The opportunity to start a business on its merits without large corporations "pulling the ladder up" behind them must return. We aren't unmindful; there is clearly much to be done.

Adam Smith proposed a way forward in the time of America's Founding Fathers that we have allowed an aristocratic class to distort into something he would certainly call an abomination. When the 'butchers, brewers and bakers' of society are Tyson, Anheuser-Busch and Nestlé, his 'Invisible Hand' gets replaced by a tight-fisted "Infinity Glove." Not that capitalism is necessarily the only path a polis can choose, but *that* is not capitalism.

In an AreTéCracy, we stop subsidizing giants and start funding the little guy. By partnering with AI and advanced tooling, we empower a new class of artisan-citizens to build, repair, and trade locally. We replace fragile supply chains with resilient, distributed workshops. It is the return of real capitalism: many hands, fair competition, and no overlords.
