AreTéCracy proposes that there should be a new guaranteed right for every citizen: That the right to “flourish as a human,” as Aristotle put it, or the “pursuit of happiness,” as Locke and Jefferson put it, must be preserved in both spirit and law. To be clear, it doesn’t stop anyone from achieving what they can on their own merits; it simply assures that what we call the Survival Stack is equally accessible to all.

We all already know what needs to end. There can never again be 'too big to fail.' The power to control the lion’s share of online commerce, one third of low-earth satellites, or the critical technologies driving the advancement of the human experience and a post-scarcity world cannot rest in the hands of a few self-interested oligarchs.

Furthermore, financial institutions must be held responsible for their actions rather than playing roulette with our deposits and depending on bailouts. The minimum standards for the Survival Stack—good healthcare, childcare, housing, food, and education—must be established and guaranteed to every citizen. Essential infrastructure must be reliably built, financed, and purposed. The opportunity to start a business on its merits, without large corporations 'pulling the ladder up' behind them, must return. We aren’t unmindful: There is clearly much to be done.

Adam Smith proposed a way forward in the time of America’s Founding Fathers that we have allowed an aristocratic class to distort into something he would certainly call an abomination. When the ‘butchers, brewers and bakers’ of society are Tyson, Anheuser-Busch and Nestlé, his ‘Invisible Hand’ gets replaced by a tight-fisted “Infinity Glove.” Not that capitalism is necessarily the only path a polis can choose, but *that* is not capitalism.

In an AreTéCracy, we stop subsidizing giants and start funding the little guy. By partnering with AI and advanced tooling, we empower a new class of artisan-citizens to build, repair, and trade locally. We replace fragile supply chains with resilient, distributed workshops. For those Ekklesias that choose *only* capitalism (most probably won't), it is the return of real capitalism: many hands, fair competition, and no overlords.
