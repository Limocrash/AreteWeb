Actual democracy should never resemble a contest, a sport, or testosterone-loaded theater. It certainly shouldn't look like WWE or early 21st century reality television, the way it often does now. Real democracy is finding consensus. It’s teamwork. There isn’t one citizen in the polis who doesn’t at least deserve to be heard and have their concerns earnestly listened to. So long as people truly believe the will of the people is what government should reflect, they have a duty to discover what the will of the people truly is. That is a core belief of AreTéCracy. 

Under that ideological framework, AreTéCracy has developed a sophisticated method for public deliberation. One of our tools is **IsoKratÓS**; our AI discourse assistant that replaces the theater and bravado with a workshop. It coaches citizens to turn ideas into proposals, and shows every citizen how to present and defend them in the arena. During deliberation, it also helps moderators enforce rules of discourse designed to keep rhetoric and debate truthful, respectful and focused on consensus. One of these is the "Steel Man Rule": you cannot criticize an argument until you can restate it to your opponent's satisfaction—a feature that eliminates strawman arguments and drastically reduces performative debate. But there is so much more.

There are rarely cases where genuine differences in views can’t be squared if every person is willing to accept every other person’s as valid. Exceptions happen when intolerant minorities have no interest in accepting the legitimacy of others or their views, typically due to religious extremism, bigotry, or anti-democratic ideology. For these attempts to subvert Homonoia, AreTéCracy has structural safeguards designed to resist and neutralize bad-faith actors.

Again, for greater detail see our full discourse on the subject in *The People's Assembly* in the Blueprint section. But understand this one thing: We don't just hope for better discourse; we’ve engineered it. That said, we understand if you’re skeptical, given the experiences you’ve been through the last couple of decades. All we can ask is that you keep your mind open to the possibilities as you explore the ideas this website presents.
