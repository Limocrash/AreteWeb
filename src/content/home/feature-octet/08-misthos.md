We understand the alternatives varying sides have proposed about what to do when advancements in AI and robotics make virtually all of the current job market obsolete. But every side has forgotten to count one essential job that no robot can ever do in a human's stead. We understand why: They don't remember it because we gave it away over 2,000 years ago. That job is self-governance.

That, of course, assumes we choose to *have* self-governance over the only real alternative. Once AI and robots can do most of what humans alone currently do in the economic sphere, representative government as we know it will cease to be relevant. We will either live in a technocracy—a new aristocracy where robot owners rule—or a real democracy, where *we* own the robots. If we choose the latter, then no AI or robot can ever do the one thing we must always do ourselves, for ourselves and by no one else: *Be* a democracy.

This civic duty creates a potential part-time or even full-time occupation for every citizen. A duty this vital deserves compensation, much like the Attic Greeks were paid two millennia ago: through *misthos*, but of a kind completely unique to AreTéCracy. This citizen compensation eliminates the need for UBI, reserving some form of that concept only for cases of extreme disability, rehabilitation, or similar incapacities.

To have that world, we must reclaim our right to self-governance instead of outsourcing it, and we must take the helm to steer our ship of state toward a future that isn’t apocalyptic. We believe it can be done, but this moment is what the ancient Greeks called *kairos*: we must act now or lose the opportunity, possibly forever.
