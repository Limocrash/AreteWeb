We understand the arguments that have been put forward, from different sides, about what we do when the advancement of AI and robotics make nearly all of the current job market obsolete. But we honestly think every one of those sides is forgetting to count the one job we gave away over 2,000 years ago that no robot can ever do in a human's stead. That job is self-governance.

That, of course, assumes we choose to *have* self-governance. Once AI and robots can do most of what humans alone currently do in the economic sphere, representative government as we know it will make no sense. We will either live in a technocracy — which is nothing more than an aristocracy, oligarchy or monarchy where those who own the robots use them to rule — or a real democracy, where *we* own the robots. If we choose democracy, then no AI or robot can ever do the one thing we must always do ourselves: *Be* a democracy. And that has the potential to create a full-time job for every citizen.

As such, it deserves to be compensated in the same way Attic Greeks did over two millennia ago: through *misthos*.
